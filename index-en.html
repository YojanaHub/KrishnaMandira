Temple Heritage

Sri Santana Gopala Krishna Swamy Temple, located in Kalmane village near Sagara in Karnataka’s Malenadu region, is a sacred center of devotion, faith, and divine blessings. The temple serves as the presiding deity of the village and is situated within the historic Agrahara established during the period of the Ikkeri rulers. This Agrahara is a holy and culturally significant site, once home to eminent scholars and renowned intellectuals such as Bhattoji Dikshita and Tirumala Bhatta.

01-05-1968

Divine Reinstallation of Lord Krishna’s Presence

Once an ancient temple in ruins, it was revived through the divine blessings of Bhagawan Sadguru Shreedhara Swamy. Swamiji offered daily naivedya to Lord Krishna from his Ashram and, with deep devotion, personally wrote a sacred Yantra, instructing the villagers to install it during the Pratishthapane. Following his guidance, devotees came together to rebuild the temple, and the Pratishthapane and Astabandha were performed, marking a blessed renewal of this holy shrine.

05-03-2003

Renovation and Shikhara Kalasha Stapane

Later, with the divine blessings of Jagadguru Shankaracharya Raghaveshwara Bharati Swamiji, the Shikhara Kalasha Pratishthapane of the newly built temple was carried out. This reconstruction was made possible through the faith and generous contributions of devotees, whose devotion helped bring this sacred vision to life.

Present

A living temple of Devotion

Today, the temple continues to radiate spiritual energy as a living center of bhakti. Daily poojas, Purusha Sookta Abhisheka, and sevas are performed with devotion. The temple also hosts numerous dharmic programmes and festivals such as Varshikotsava, Satyanarayana Kathe, Devi Mahatme, Krishna Moola Mantra Havana, Upakarma, Krishnashtami, and Pallakki Utsava, drawing devotees from far and wide.

Over the years, countless devotees have offered prayers here for health, prosperity, and children, and many have experienced the grace of Lord Santana Gopala Krishna in their lives.
